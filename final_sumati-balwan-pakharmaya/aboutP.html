ABOUT US

Dr. (Mrs.) Sharayu Ghole, a dedicated gynecologist, has been running a hospital in
Dhankawadi, Pune, since 1977. Inspired by the teachings of Swami Vivekananda
and driven by a deep sense of gratitude to society, Dr. Ghole, along with
Dr. M.N. Hardikar and other like-minded individuals, came together with a vision
to make a meaningful contribution to society.

This collective effort led to the establishment of the "Radhabai Hardikar Pranijat
Mangal Sanstha" Trust. The journey began modestly over 30 years ago with the
opening of a small dispensary and hospital in Nimbalkarwadi-Gujarwadi, then
small hamlets under Katraj Gram Panchayat. The location was chosen due to the
lack of medical facilities and the absence of public transport, addressing a critical

Need in the community. Encouraged by the success of these initiatives, the Trust
took a further step in 1999 by founding an orphanage with a difference. Unlike
traditional institutions, this orphanage is designed to provide a true "Home"
for the 25 children who live there, ensuring they grow up in a nurturing and
supportive environment. To provide these children, and others from nearby

villages, with a quality education, the Trust established a charitable school,
“Sumati Balwan,” in 2001. This school, which currently educates 650 students
from Kindergarten to 10th grade, offers a unique learning experience. The
focus is on holistic education, where children learn amidst nature,

Through play, dance, singing, and by discovering and nurturing their innate
talents. Additionally, to equip students with practical skills, the school
introduced the Introduction to Basic Technology (IBT) program for
students from 8th to 10th grade,
fostering independence and
self-sufficiency.
